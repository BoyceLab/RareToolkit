# Consent, privacy, and trust

Strong data collection depends on trust.

## Questions to settle early

- What data are you collecting and why?
- Who can access identifiable data?
- What gets shared externally?
- Can participants withdraw?
- Can they update consent preferences?
- Will they be recontacted for future studies?
- Will genetic reports be uploaded and curated?

## Plain-language consent principles

Your materials should clearly explain:

- purpose of the registry,
- expected uses of data,
- limits of privacy protection,
- data retention approach,
- return of results or updates policy,
- participant choices.

## Trust-building practices

- publish a plain-language FAQ,
- explain why each sensitive field is needed,
- let participants skip nonessential questions,
- show how data are protected,
- report back to the community about registry impact.
