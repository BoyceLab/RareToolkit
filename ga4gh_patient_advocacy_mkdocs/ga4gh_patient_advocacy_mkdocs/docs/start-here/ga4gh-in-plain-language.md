# GA4GH in plain language

## What is GA4GH?

GA4GH stands for the **Global Alliance for Genomics and Health**. It develops standards and policy frameworks so health and genomics data can be used more consistently and shared more responsibly.

For patient advocacy groups, GA4GH matters because it offers a path from local registry data to broader interoperability.

## What GA4GH is not

GA4GH is not a single registry platform, not a hospital database, and not a patient portal. Think of it as a standards ecosystem.

## GA4GH standards most relevant to patient registries

### Phenopackets
A way to package information about a person or biosample, including disease, phenotypic features, measurements, interpretations, and more.

### Pedigree
Useful when family structure is important to understanding inheritance patterns.

### Policy and framework resources
Helpful when your group is thinking about consent, data sharing, and responsible governance.

## Why advocacy groups should care

Using GA4GH-aligned structures can help your registry data become easier to:

- combine with partner datasets,
- support case matching,
- travel with participants across studies,
- and contribute to research and variant interpretation efforts.

## A realistic adoption pattern

Most advocacy groups should not try to implement every standard at once.

A manageable sequence is:

1. build plain-language questionnaires,
2. map phenotype items to HPO,
3. standardize disease and test-result fields,
4. create a data dictionary,
5. export core records into Phenopacket-like structures.
