# A simple adoption roadmap

## Phase 1: Stabilize core collection

Create a core intake survey with fields for:

- participant identifiers used internally,
- diagnosis and suspected diagnosis,
- genetic testing status,
- uploaded report availability,
- phenotype by body system,
- age of onset,
- current treatments,
- family history,
- demographics important to your program.

## Phase 2: Add structured phenotype mapping

Map answer options to:

- HPO terms,
- disease identifiers,
- optional onset categories,
- provenance fields showing who reported the information and when.

## Phase 3: Build update workflows

Add:

- annual update survey,
- event-triggered updates after major test results,
- medication and outcome updates,
- report re-upload or amendment workflows.

## Phase 4: Export and share responsibly

Prepare standardized exports for research or partner exchange. This is where Phenopackets become especially useful.

## Phase 5: Improve quality over time

Review:

- missingness,
- drop-off by section,
- unclear wording,
- overuse of free text,
- HPO mapping gaps,
- participant burden.
