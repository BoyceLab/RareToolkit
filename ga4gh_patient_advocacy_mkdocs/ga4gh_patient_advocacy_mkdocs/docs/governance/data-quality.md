# Data quality and updates

## Quality improves when forms are designed for reality

Rare disease data are often incomplete, uncertain, and changing. Good registries are built to handle that.

## Recommended practices

- allow “unknown” and “unsure,”
- keep branching logic simple,
- review unmapped free text regularly,
- version your data dictionary,
- track who supplied each data point,
- re-collect key fields over time.

## Useful quality metrics

- completion rate by section,
- percentage of phenotype items mapped to HPO,
- report upload rate,
- annual update completion rate,
- rate of missing onset data,
- number of free-text entries later converted into structured fields.
